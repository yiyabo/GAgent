Cell-Cell Communication Analysis: CellChat Methodology
======================================================

Dataset and Sample Composition
------------------------------
Single-cell RNA sequencing data were obtained from the Gene Expression Omnibus
(GEO) under accession GSE184880. The dataset comprises 12 samples: 7 ovarian
cancer (OC) tumor samples and 5 normal (non-malignant) tissue samples.

Quality Control and Preprocessing
----------------------------------
Individual cells were subjected to the following quality control filters prior
to downstream analysis:
  - Minimum UMI counts per cell: 500
  - Maximum UMI counts per cell: 50,000
  - Minimum genes detected per cell: 200
  - Maximum genes detected per cell: 6,000
  - Maximum mitochondrial gene percentage: 20%

Following QC filtering, batch effect correction was performed using the
Harmony algorithm. After Harmony integration, a total of 211,163 cells were
retained for downstream analysis.

Cell Type Annotation
--------------------
Eight major cell types were identified and annotated:
  Epithelial, Fibroblast, Macrophage, T_cell, NK, Endothelial, Pericyte,
  and Proliferating.

Group Comparisons
-----------------
Three experimental groups were defined for comparative analysis:
  - N: Normal tissue
  - OC: Ovarian cancer tumor tissue
  - MT: Metastatic/other tissue

The primary analysis focus was the OC group, specifically examining
ligand-receptor interactions between malignant epithelial tumor cells and
tumor microenvironment (TME) populations.

CellChat Framework
------------------
Cell-cell communication inference was performed using the CellChat framework
(Jin et al., 2021), which models intercellular signaling through curated
ligand-receptor (LR) interaction databases.

Ligand-Receptor Database (CellChatDB):
CellChatDB provides a comprehensive, manually curated collection of LR pairs
compiled from multiple biological databases and literature sources, including
KEGG, Reactome, IntAct, and PubMed-annotated publications. The database
encompasses signaling pathways, secreted factors, extracellular matrix
interactions, and cell-surface receptor-ligand pairs.

Communication Probability Computation:
CellChat computes the communication probability for each LR pair between
cell types using a mass-action model combined with a Hill-function response.
Gene expression levels of ligands and receptors are transformed through the
Hill function to estimate interaction strength. The model accounts for
multi-subunit receptor complexes, where multiple gene products must be
co-expressed to form a functional receptor complex. For each pair of cell
types, the communication probability P is computed as the product of
normalized expression levels of the ligand in the source cell population
and the receptor in the target cell population, modulated by the Hill
function response curve.

Pathway-Level Aggregation:
Individual LR pair communication probabilities are aggregated to the
pathway level by summing probabilities across all LR pairs belonging to
the same signaling pathway. This yields a pathway-level communication
score that reflects the total signaling strength between two cell types
via a specific biological pathway. Contribution analysis further identifies
the relative contribution of individual LR pairs within each pathway.

Statistical Testing
--------------------
Statistical significance of inferred communication events was assessed
using a permutation-based approach. Specifically, 1,000 random permutations
of cell labels were performed to generate an empirical null distribution
of communication probabilities under the hypothesis of no structured
cell-type-specific signaling. Observed communication probabilities were
compared against this null distribution to compute empirical p-values.

Multiple testing correction was applied using the Benjamini-Hochberg
false discovery rate (FDR) procedure. Interactions with an adjusted
p-value (adj-p) less than 0.05 were considered statistically significant.

Analysis Scope
--------------
The primary analytical objective was to reconstruct and characterize the
cell-cell communication network within the tumor microenvironment of
ovarian cancer, with emphasis on signaling axes between malignant epithelial
tumor cells and stromal and immune TME populations (fibroblasts,
macrophages, T cells, NK cells, and endothelial cells) in the OC group.
