# Phage Taxonomy Reference

## ICTV Classification System (2023 Release - Major Reorganization)

### ⚠️ IMPORTANT: ICTV 2022-2023 Taxonomy Reform

**ICTV has reorganized Caudovirales into 20+ families based on phylogenomics.**
The traditional morphology-based families (Myoviridae, Siphoviridae, Podoviridae) have been **dissolved**.

**New classification approach**:
- **Phylogenomics-based**: Families now defined by shared gene content and evolutionary history
- **Morphology is secondary**: Tail type is descriptive but not taxonomic
- **Legacy terms**: Still used informally for morphological descriptions

**This document covers both**:
1. **Modern ICTV 2023 families** (primary classification)
2. **Legacy morphology terms** (for historical context and morphological descriptions)

---

### Order Caudovirales (Tailed Phages)

**Characteristics**: dsDNA genomes, tailed virions, ~96% of all known phages

## Modern ICTV 2023 Families (Phylogenomics-Based)

### Family Herelleviridae (T4-like phages)

**Morphology**: Long contractile tails (80-455 nm), head diameter 50-110 nm

**Genome Characteristics**:
- Size: 33-500 kb (typical 160-170 kb)
- GC content: 35-55%
- Genes: 50-700 ORFs
- Modified bases: Often hydroxymethylcytosine (T4-like)

**Key Genera**:

#### Genus Tequatrovirus (T4-like)
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus T4
- **Genome**: 169 kb, 289 genes, GC 35%
- **Key markers**:
  - gp23: Major capsid protein (HK97-fold)
  - gp18: Tail sheath protein (contractile)
  - gp19: Tail tube protein
  - gp24: Vertex protein
  - gp37/gp38: Long tail fibers (host recognition)
  - gp5: Lysozyme (baseplate)
- **Host range**: Enterobacteria (E. coli, Salmonella, Shigella)
- **Replication**: Lytic only, no integration
- **DNA modification**: Glucosylated hydroxymethylcytosine (HMC)

#### Genus Tequintavirus (T5-like)
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus T5
- **Genome**: 121 kb, 168 genes, GC 39%
- **Key markers**:
  - Pre-early genes: Transcribed before DNA replication
  - L-shaped tail fibers: Receptor binding (FhuA)
- **Unique feature**: Two-step DNA injection (8% then 92%)

#### Genus Felixounavirus (Felix O1-like)
- **Type species**: Salmonella virus FelixO1
- **Genome**: 86 kb, 131 genes, GC 38%
- **Host**: Salmonella (broad host range)
- **Application**: Biocontrol agent

#### Genus Kayvirus (Staphylococcus phages)
- **Type species**: Staphylococcus virus K
- **Genome**: 138 kb, 217 genes, GC 30%
- **Host**: Staphylococcus aureus (including MRSA)
- **Key feature**: Twiddle fibers (host recognition)

#### Genus Viunavirus (ViI-like)
- **Type species**: Salmonella virus ViI
- **Genome**: 159 kb, 248 genes, GC 42%
- **Host**: Salmonella Typhi (Vi antigen)

### Family Drexlerviridae (λ-like phages)

**Morphology**: Long non-contractile tails (100-600 nm), head diameter 40-70 nm

**Genome Characteristics**:
- Size: 17-150 kb (typical 40-60 kb)
- GC content: 35-70%
- Genes: 25-250 ORFs
- Lifestyle: Often temperate (lysogenic)

**Key Genera**:

#### Genus Lambdavirus (λ-like)
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus lambda
- **Genome**: 48.5 kb, 66 genes, GC 51%
- **Key markers**:
  - int: Integrase (tyrosine recombinase, attP/attB)
  - CI: λ repressor (helix-turn-helix, maintains lysogeny)
  - CII/CIII: Lysogeny establishment
  - Cro: Lytic switch (antagonizes CI)
  - N/Q: Antitermination proteins
  - O/P: DNA replication initiation
  - S/R: Lysis (holin/endolysin)
- **Lifecycle**: Temperate (lysogenic or lytic decision)
- **Integration**: attB site (between gal and bio operons)
- **Induction**: SOS response → RecA* → CI cleavage

#### Genus HK97virus
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus HK97
- **Genome**: 40 kb, 61 genes, GC 57%
- **Key feature**: Head-to-tail joining (covalent cross-links)
- **Capsid**: HK97-fold (chainmail topology)

#### Genus N15virus
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus N15
- **Genome**: 47 kb, 53 genes, GC 42%
- **Unique feature**: Linear plasmid prophage (telN telomerase)
- **Replication**: Circular intermediate → linear plasmid

#### Genus Phietavirus (Φ29-like)
- **Type species**: Bacillus virus phi29
- **Genome**: 19 kb, 30 genes, GC 40%
- **Key marker**: Protein-primed DNA replication (gp3 terminal protein)
- **Packaging**: Headful mechanism with pRNA

#### Genus C2virus (Lactococcus phages)
- **Type species**: Lactococcus virus c2
- **Genome**: 27 kb, 44 genes, GC 36%
- **Host**: Lactococcus lactis (dairy industry)
- **Impact**: Cheese starter culture failures

#### Genus L5virus (Mycobacterium phages)
- **Type species**: Mycobacterium virus L5
- **Genome**: 52 kb, 84 genes, GC 67%
- **Host**: Mycobacterium smegmatis, M. tuberculosis
- **Integration**: attB site (tRNA-Gly)
- **Application**: TB diagnostics and therapy

### Family Rountreeviridae (T7-like phages)

**Morphology**: Short non-contractile tails (10-20 nm), head diameter 45-70 nm

**Genome Characteristics**:
- Size: 18-100 kb (typical 40-75 kb)
- GC content: 40-60%
- Genes: 20-120 ORFs
- Lifestyle: Mostly lytic

**Key Genera**:

#### Genus T7virus (T7-like)
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus T7
- **Genome**: 40 kb, 55 genes, GC 48%
- **Key markers**:
  - Gene 1: T7 RNA polymerase (single-subunit)
  - Gene 5: DNA polymerase (thioredoxin binding)
  - Gene 10: Major capsid protein
  - Gene 17.5: Holin (lysis)
  - Gene 18: Tail spike (LPS degradation)
- **Replication**: Lytic, rapid (25 min latent period)
- **Transcription**: Phage RNA polymerase (shuts off host)

#### Genus P22virus (Salmonella phages)
- **Type species**: Salmonella virus P22
- **Genome**: 42 kb, 62 genes, GC 52%
- **Key feature**: Generalized transduction (host DNA packaging)
- **Integration**: attB site (tRNA-Leu)
- **Lysis**: Lysis from without (high MOI)

#### Genus Epsilon15virus (Epsilon15-like)
- **Type species**: Salmonella virus epsilon15
- **Genome**: 39 kb, 52 genes, GC 49%
- **Host**: Salmonella (O-antigen conversion)
- **Tail spike**: O-antigen depolymerase

#### Genus Phi29virus (Bacillus phages)
- **Type species**: Bacillus virus phi29
- **Genome**: 19 kb, 30 genes, GC 40%
- **Key feature**: Protein-primed DNA replication
- **Packaging**: pRNA-dependent (unique mechanism)
- **Application**: Nanotechnology (pRNA scaffolds)

#### Genus Luz24virus (Pseudomonas phages)
- **Type species**: Pseudomonas virus LUZ24
- **Genome**: 73 kb, 96 genes, GC 55%
- **Host**: Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- **Application**: Phage therapy (cystic fibrosis)

## Legacy Morphology Terms (Historical Context)

**Note**: These terms are **no longer valid for taxonomic classification** but are still used informally to describe morphology.

### Myoviridae Morphology (Contractile Tails)
- **Now split into**: Herelleviridae, Peduoviridae, Sphaerolipoviridae, etc.
- **Morphology**: Long contractile tails (80-455 nm)
- **Use case**: Morphological descriptions only, not taxonomy

### Siphoviridae Morphology (Long Non-Contractile Tails)
- **Now split into**: Drexlerviridae, Siphoviridae sensu stricto, Naomviridae, etc.
- **Morphology**: Long non-contractile tails (100-600 nm)
- **Use case**: Morphological descriptions only, not taxonomy

### Podoviridae Morphology (Short Non-Contractile Tails)
- **Now split into**: Rountreeviridae, Autographiviridae, Zobellviridae, etc.
- **Morphology**: Short non-contractile tails (10-20 nm)
- **Use case**: Morphological descriptions only, not taxonomy

### Other Phage Orders

#### Order Tubulavirales (Filamentous Phages)
**Family Inoviridae**:
- **Morphology**: Filamentous (6 nm × 900-2000 nm)
- **Genome**: ssDNA, 5-10 kb
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus M13
- **Key features**:
  - Secretion without lysis (chronic infection)
  - pIII/pVI/pVIII: Coat proteins
  - pII: Replication initiator (rolling circle)
  - pV: ssDNA binding protein
- **Applications**: Phage display (Smith, 2018 Nobel Prize)

#### Order Levivirales (ssRNA Phages)
**Family Leviviridae**:
- **Morphology**: Icosahedral (26 nm diameter)
- **Genome**: (+)ssRNA, 3.5-4.2 kb
- **Type species**: Escherichia virus MS2
- **Key features**:
  - 4 genes: Maturation (A), coat, lysis, replicase (β)
  - Coat protein: Translational repressor (binds TR stem-loop)
  - Lysis: L protein (inhibits MraY, cell wall synthesis)
- **Applications**: RNA phage display, MS2-tagging

#### Order Corticovirales (Lipid-Containing Phages)
**Family Corticoviridae**:
- **Type species**: Pseudomonas virus PRD1
- **Genome**: dsDNA, 15 kb
- **Key feature**: Internal lipid membrane (protein-encapsidated)
- **Capsid**: Pseudo T=25 icosahedral (P3 protein)

## Taxonomic Assignment Methods

### 1. BLAST-Based Assignment

**Marker Gene**: Terminase large subunit (TerL)
- **Why TerL?**: Conserved, single-copy, essential for packaging
- **Thresholds**:
  - Same species: >95% ANI (whole genome) or >98% TerL identity
  - Same genus: 70-95% TerL identity
  - Same family: 40-70% TerL identity
  - Different family: <40% TerL identity

**Command**:
```bash
blastp -query phage_terl.fasta -db phage_terl_db -evalue 1e-10 -outfmt 6
```

### 2. vConTACT2 (Network-Based)

**Method**: Gene-sharing networks (protein clusters)
- **Advantage**: Handles novel phages without close relatives
- **Output**: Viral clusters (VCs) at genus/family level
- **Confidence**: Intra-cluster score >0.5 = same genus

**Command**:
```bash
vcontact2 --raw-proteins phage_proteins.faa --rels-mode Diamond --db Prokka-viral --pcs-mode MCL --vcs-mode ClusterONE
```

### 3. CheckV (Quality Assessment)

**Method**: Hidden Markov models for viral genes
- **Completeness**: % of expected viral genes present
- **Contamination**: Multiple copies of single-copy genes
- **Quality tiers**:
  - Complete: 100% completeness, no contamination
  - High-quality: >90% completeness, <5% contamination
  - Medium-quality: 50-90% completeness
  - Low-quality: <50% completeness

**Command**:
```bash
checkv end_to_end phage_genomes.fna checkv_output -t 8
```

### 4. PhaGC (Phage Genome Classification)

**Method**: Deep learning on k-mer frequencies
- **Input**: Whole genome sequence
- **Output**: Family-level classification
- **Accuracy**: >90% for Caudovirales families

**Command**:
```bash
phagc predict -i phage_genome.fna -o phagc_output
```

## Common Misclassifications

### 1. Siphoviridae vs Myoviridae
**Problem**: Long-tailed phages misclassified
**Cause**: Tail contraction not visible in TEM
**Solution**: Check for tail sheath genes (gp18 homolog)

### 2. Podoviridae vs Siphoviridae
**Problem**: Short-tailed siphoviruses called podoviruses
**Cause**: Tail length <50 nm (borderline)
**Solution**: Check for RNA/DNA polymerase (Podoviridae-specific)

### 3. Temperate vs Lytic
**Problem**: Temperate phages called lytic
**Cause**: Integrase too divergent for BLAST
**Solution**: HMM search (PF00589, PF02899) with e-value <1e-3

### 4. Novel Phages
**Problem**: No BLAST hits to known phages
**Cause**: Truly novel or incomplete database
**Solution**: Use vConTACT2 (network-based) or PhaGC (deep learning)
